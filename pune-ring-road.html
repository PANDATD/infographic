Dehu Road–Narhe Elevated Corridor

A Strategic Lifeline to Power Pune’s Future-Ready Transport Network

The Problem: Chronic Congestion

Pune's NH-48 is a daily choke point, mixing highway and city traffic. This cripples commutes, hurts the economy, and impacts Hinjewadi IT Park, the engine of Maharashtra's IT exports.

The Solution: An Elevated Corridor

A 32 km elevated expressway to separate long-distance vehicles from local commuters, creating a two-tier system for smooth, fast, and efficient travel across the city.

Project at a Glance

32 km Total Length
₹6,000 Cr Estimated Cost
2 Phases Execution Plan
NHAI Executing Body

Pune's Integrated 3-Tier Transport Network

Tier 1: Elevated Corridor

A high-speed link for traffic INSIDE the city.

Tier 2: Ring Road (173 km)

A massive bypass to divert traffic AROUND the city.

Tier 3: Greenfield Expressway (700 km)

A new alignment for seamless, high-speed travel BETWEEN cities (Pune-Bengaluru).

Boosting Real Estate

Enhanced connectivity is set to multiply property values, with areas near the Ring Road already seeing 12-18% annual growth. This will drive organized, sustainable urban expansion.

Unlocking Economic Potential

As part of the Bharatmala Pariyojana, the corridor will improve logistics, attract new industries, create thousands of jobs, and cement Pune's status as a global business hub.

This project isn't just building a road; it's paving the way for a smarter, faster, and more prosperous Pune.
